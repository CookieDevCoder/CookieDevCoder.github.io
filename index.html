MineSweeper

I recreated the Game "MineSweeper" in unity. When you tap on the first tile, the mines are generated. they can generate in every tile exept the tile you initially clicked on. Afterwards, we check how many mines are surrounding the initial click. After calculating that, it will open all tiles that contain 0 mines around itself. any tiles that contain a mine around it will show the number of mines that surround that tile. You are able to flag tiles. the game is complete once all tiles exept for flagged tiles are uncovered. You lose if you click on a mine tile.

Chess

I recreated the Chess Board game. There are a couple of different pieces that can move in various ways. These include the Pawn, Bishop, Knight, Rook, King, and Queen. The pawn can only move forward and capture diagnally one tile. The Bishop can move to any unblocked diagnal tile The Knight can Jump over pieces and follows the pattern of moving North East South or West 3 tiles and 1 tile to the right or left relative to the direction moved. The Knight is not blocked by pieces that are in the way to the target tile. The Rook can move to any unblocked tile Vertically or Horizontally The Queen can move Vertically, Horizontally, or diagnally to any unblocked tile. The king can move to any of the 8 tiles surrounding the king exept if that tile is guarded by an enemy piece. If the enenmy provides a check, The king must be protected in some way during the move.

CookieCraft

CookieCraft is one of my Major projects on Scratch.
 In this project, I made a sort of Mini RPG game. The game itself is not very long, but the code behind it was the best at the time of publishing
Unfortunately, I stopped working on it after realizing it would be too much work to make a major game like this one on such a limited "Game Engine".

Some of the features include scrolling through the world. Even today, many who use scratch avoid making a scrolling video game. Many resort to having a static level and teleporting you between them.
In my game, the camera follows where the player goes. Basically what I did was make every object have an x and y private variable. Using this private variable, I would just subtract their (x, y) coordinates from the Player (x, y) coordinates. This would then keep the offset as the player walks around the world.
Other than that, the game has a Sniper Rifle for a weapon. You can use it to shoot at an evil raisin cookie. You also have access to the Dead Eye ability from Read Dead Redemption.
You have two NPCs you can talk to. The first one you talk to is Dumbodoor, a cookie version of dumbledoar. The second one is a Dinosaur. This dinosaur asks you questions. You have to respond with the correct answer, but answer wrong and he eats you. After the quiz, he praises you before he dashes at you and tries to attack. You are forced into deadeye and you must shoot him.
Unfortunately, There is no ending, and Once the quiz is beaten, the dinosaur stays and is completely harmless. There is also no way to kill it. Despite that, you can now go back to dumbodoor and talk to him about the dinosaur population.
